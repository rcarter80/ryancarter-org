Hack Music LA Portfolio

iMonkeypants

iMonkeypants is an iOS app album that I built. The music is synthesized in real time and responds to accelerometer data, so moving your device will sculpt the sound as you listen. I've presented iMonkeypants at the Center for Computer Research in Music and Acoustics at Stanford University, the Computer Music Center at Columbia University, and the National Conference of the Society for Electro-Acoustic Music in the United States. iMonkeypants is available for download from the App Store.

Headless Monkey Attack

Headless Monkey Attack is a live electronic performance project using the same programming language as iMonkeypants in conjunction with a video game controller (the GameTrak "tether" controller). Described by Allan Kozinn of the Wall Street Journal as "a vivid, driven score for electronics and drums", Latency in the System is the debut album, which I've presented at the International Computer Music Conference and the Center for New Music and Audio Technologies at the University of California, Berkeley.

I'm currently producing a second album for a future tour. Source code and additional information can be found on the Headless Monkey Attack site.

The Clock Behind Me

The Clock Behind Me is an orchestral work inspired by this interview with my first composition teacher, Richard Hoffmann, who came to LA to study with Arnold Schoenberg and who cared for Schoenberg at the end of his life.

The recording was produced live at the 2015 EARSHOT BERKELEY UNDER CONSTRUCTION NEW MUSIC READINGS, in partnership with the American Composers Orchestra

performed by the Berkeley Symphony; Joana Carneiro, conductor

On the expressive potential of suboptimal speakers

On the expressive potential of suboptimal speakers is a recently completed concert work for motion-controlled interactive electronics synthesized on the mobile devices of the audience. This is a piece for audience participation only, with no additional performers. My goal is to further develop ideas that I first explored when building iMonkeypants, with an emphasis on increased accessibility. This web-based solution allows anyone with a mobile device to simply navigate to the page and have direct, physical control over the music without any additional hardware or software.
